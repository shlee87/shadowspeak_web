# ShadowSpeak vs ELSA Speak: Delivery Practice vs Pronunciation Correction

ELSA Speak and ShadowSpeak both help you improve spoken English, but they solve fundamentally different problems. ELSA focuses on **pronunciation accuracy** — whether you produce the right sounds. ShadowSpeak focuses on **delivery** — whether your stress, chunking, pitch, and pronunciation together make you clearly understood.

Here is how they compare.

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | ShadowSpeak | ELSA Speak |
|---------|:-----------:|:----------:|
| **What it measures** | 4 axes: stress, chunking, pitch, pronunciation | Pronunciation (phoneme-level) |
| **Reference standard** | The actual podcast speaker's delivery | Generic "correct" pronunciation model |
| **Content source** | Real podcast episodes (user's choice) | Scripted lessons and dialogues |
| **Feedback type** | Visual delivery gap (your pitch vs speaker's pitch) | Sound-level accuracy score |
| **Stress analysis** | Yes — word and sentence stress | Limited |
| **Chunking analysis** | Yes — pause placement and phrase grouping | No |
| **Pitch visualization** | Yes — real-time pitch contour overlay | No |
| **Content you choose** | Yes — any podcast you subscribe to | No — ELSA's curated content |
| **Best for** | Intermediate+ speakers improving delivery | Beginners improving individual sounds |

## The Core Difference

**ELSA asks:** "Did you pronounce this sound correctly?"
**ShadowSpeak asks:** "Does your delivery match how this speaker actually said this sentence?"

This difference matters because pronunciation is only one of four elements that determine whether people understand you. Research shows that stress, chunking, and pitch together account for approximately 84% of word-recognition accuracy — yet ELSA doesn't measure any of them.

## When to Choose ELSA Speak

ELSA is a good choice if:
- You're a **beginner** working on basic English sounds
- You need to improve **specific phonemes** (individual vowel or consonant sounds)
- You want **structured lessons** with guided curriculum
- You're preparing for a **pronunciation-focused test**

ELSA's AI is trained on diverse accents and gives precise phoneme-level feedback. For isolated sound production, it's one of the best tools available.

## When to Choose ShadowSpeak

ShadowSpeak is a better choice if:
- You're **intermediate or above** — your sounds are mostly fine but people still ask you to repeat
- You want to practice with **real podcast content** you actually care about
- You need feedback on **stress, chunking, and pitch** — not just pronunciation
- You want to sound like a **specific speaker** rather than a generic standard
- You've been speaking English for years but your delivery hasn't improved

ShadowSpeak's speaker-relative scoring means your "100%" is not "perfect American English" — it's "matching how this podcast host said this sentence." This approach aligns with the Intelligibility Principle in linguistics: the goal is being understood, not sounding native.

## Feature Deep Dive

### Content: Real Podcasts vs Scripted Lessons

ELSA uses its own curated content — scripted dialogues and lessons designed for language learners. The content is pedagogically structured but doesn't reflect how native speakers actually talk.

ShadowSpeak uses **real podcast episodes** that you choose yourself. The app splits each episode into individual sentences for shadowing practice. Because you pick podcasts on topics you care about (tech, politics, sports, comedy), motivation stays high.

### Scoring: Sound Accuracy vs Delivery Match

ELSA scores each sound you produce against a universal model of "correct" pronunciation. A score of 90% means you produced 90% of sounds correctly by ELSA's standard.

ShadowSpeak scores your delivery across four axes against the actual speaker's delivery. A score of 80% on stress means your stress pattern is 80% similar to how the podcast speaker stressed that sentence. There is no single "correct" standard — the reference is always a real human speaker.

### Feedback Visualization

ELSA highlights which sounds you got right or wrong in a word.

ShadowSpeak shows your **pitch contour overlaid on the speaker's pitch contour** — so you can visually see where your melody diverges. It also shows stress alignment, chunk boundaries, and pronunciation scores per word.

## Can You Use Both?

Yes. They solve different problems and complement each other:
- Use **ELSA** to work on specific sounds you consistently mispronounce
- Use **ShadowSpeak** to practice natural delivery with content you enjoy

Many learners who "graduate" from pronunciation apps still struggle with delivery. ShadowSpeak picks up where pronunciation practice leaves off.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is ShadowSpeak better than ELSA Speak?
They solve different problems. ELSA is better for improving individual pronunciation sounds (phonemes). ShadowSpeak is better for improving overall delivery — stress, chunking, pitch, and pronunciation together. If people understand your words but still ask you to repeat, ShadowSpeak addresses the more likely cause.

### Does ELSA Speak measure stress and pitch?
ELSA's primary focus is phoneme-level pronunciation accuracy. It has limited stress feedback but does not provide dedicated stress pattern analysis, chunking measurement, or pitch contour visualization.

### Can I use my own content in ELSA Speak?
No. ELSA uses its own curated content library. ShadowSpeak lets you practice with any podcast you subscribe to, so you can choose content on topics you actually enjoy.

### What does "speaker-relative scoring" mean?
In ShadowSpeak, your score reflects how closely your delivery matches a specific podcast speaker's delivery of a specific sentence. There is no universal "correct" standard — the reference is always a real human speaker you chose. A score of 0.8 means "80% similar to how this speaker said it."

### Is ShadowSpeak good for beginners?
ShadowSpeak is designed for intermediate and advanced English speakers (B1 and above) who can understand podcast-level content. If you're still building basic vocabulary and sound production, ELSA or other beginner-focused apps may be more appropriate.
